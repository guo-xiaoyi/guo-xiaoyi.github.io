Projects Related to PhD

(Ongoing) Evolution of Reference Points over Time

The formation and evolution of reference points in a dynamic setting are rare topics in discussions of reference-dependent preferences. Based on existing theories, we conduct a controlled economic experiment on a representative sample. Our aim is to establish a unified framework describing reference points in dynamic settings.

Evaluating Home Effect for Professional Cycling

This research is the first in the field trying to comprehensively understand the home effect in professional cycling, based on Tour de France. We use modified causal forests to estimate the home effects of French riders in different stages. A considerable negative home effect is observed, with heterogeneous variations across stages.

coolTree: Enhanced Experimental Interface for oTree

This is a side project I built to improve the usability of oTree, a Python framework for economic experiments. coolTree provides new templates for faster setup and better visual clarity, helping researchers without advanced frontend skills. The project is open-source and in active development.
